# Market research

The evidence-gathering leg of `competitive-intel`. Feeds `analytical-frameworks`.

## Market sizing: TAM / SAM / SOM

- **TAM** total addressable market · **SAM** serviceable (your segment/geo) · **SOM** obtainable (realistic
  near-term share).
- **Use two methods and reconcile them:** *top-down* (industry figure → filter to your slice) and
  *bottom-up* (units × price × adoption). Bottom-up is usually more defensible; top-down is the sanity check.
  If they disagree wildly, your assumptions are the story.

## Sources

- **Secondary** (fast, second-hand, can be dated): filings/10-Ks, analyst reports, pricing pages, **job
  postings** (signal tech + expansion), review sites, patents, product docs, press.
- **Primary** (slower, original signal): customer/expert interviews, surveys, your own product/usage data.

## Source credibility — weigh every source

- **Who** produced it and **why**? Vendor-sponsored ≠ neutral. **How recent**? Is the sample/methodology
  sound?
- **Triangulate:** one source is a *lead*; two independent sources is a *finding*.

## Competitor profile

Build the standard profile (positioning, pricing, segment, GTM, strengths/weaknesses, recent moves,
signals) — see [`reference.md`](reference.md).

## Rules

- **Date every data point** and note its source and bias.
- **Ranges, not false precision.** Show the assumptions behind an estimate.
- Ethics per `competitive-intel`: public, lawful sources only.
