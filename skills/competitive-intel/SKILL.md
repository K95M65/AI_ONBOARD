# Competitive intelligence

The umbrella methodology. It orchestrates the specialists: `market-research` to
gather, `analytical-frameworks` to analyze, and
`data-storytelling` + `dataviz` to communicate — all under an ethics
constraint throughout.

## When to use

Answering a competitive or market question that informs a real decision.

## The cycle

1. **Scope.** What decision does this inform? Write the key intelligence question(s). Vague questions produce
   vague intel — "should we enter market X?" beats "tell me about market X."
2. **Collect.** Gather from ethical, lawful, public sources (`market-research`). Record source, date, and a
   credibility note for every data point.
3. **Analyze.** Apply the fitting framework(s) (`analytical-frameworks`). Separate observation from
   inference; attach a confidence level.
4. **Synthesize.** The "so what?" — implications and options for the decision, not a data dump.
5. **Communicate.** Lead with the answer; support with evidence and visuals (`data-storytelling`).

## Ethics — non-negotiable

- **Public, lawfully obtained information only.** No pretexting, no impersonation, no inducing anyone to
  breach an NDA or confidentiality.
- **Respect IP, privacy, and terms of service.** Don't scrape or access what you're forbidden to.
- **Fact vs. inference.** Never fabricate, and never launder a guess as a finding.
- **The publish test:** if a collection method would embarrass you if it were made public, don't use it.

## Rules

- **Decision-first.** Intel with no decision attached is trivia.
- **Date and cite everything** — markets move; stale intel misleads.
- **Flag confidence and gaps** explicitly; a known unknown is more useful than false certainty.
