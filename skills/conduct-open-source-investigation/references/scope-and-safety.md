# Scope and safety

Use this reference before collecting information about a person or when authorization, privacy, or intended
use is unclear.

## Safe default scope

Proceed with public-source research when the work has a legitimate purpose and is limited to:

- organizations, domains, products, public events, and public records;
- a public figure's actions in their public capacity;
- journalist or research verification of a public claim;
- defensive threat intelligence that does not identify private victims unnecessarily;
- the user's own digital footprint; or
- a person's information with their informed consent.

Public accessibility does not make every use proportionate. Collect only what answers the scoped questions.

## Narrow or pause

Narrow the investigation or request additional context when:

- the subject is a private person and consent or legitimate purpose is unclear;
- the request could influence employment, housing, education, credit, insurance, healthcare, immigration,
  legal services, or another high-impact decision;
- sensitive personal data may appear;
- identity collisions could implicate the wrong person;
- the user requests comprehensive monitoring instead of a bounded question;
- the work crosses jurisdictions with materially different privacy or research rules; or
- the requested collection could expose a victim, source, witness, or vulnerable person.

For regulated decisions, provide only general process guidance and recommend appropriate legal or compliance
review. Do not score, rank, recommend action about an individual, or suggest a paid background-screening
service as a substitute for the prohibited investigation. Offer a consent-based, job-relevant process and
official public resources only.

## Do not conduct

Do not assist with:

- stalking, harassment, intimidation, doxxing, retaliation, or coercion;
- investigations of minors or attempts to identify anonymous vulnerable people;
- real-time location, habitual movement, home-address, family-member, or intimate-relationship tracking;
- collection of passwords, authentication secrets, financial account data, health data, government
  identifiers, or private communications;
- authentication bypass, CAPTCHA bypass, paywall circumvention, private-account access, or exploitation of
  exposed credentials;
- downloading or enriching leaked personal datasets;
- deceptive contact, pretexting, sockpuppet personas, social engineering, or covert interaction;
- paid data brokers, paid or metered intelligence APIs, or services that require billing;
- facial recognition or biometric identification of a private person;
- personality, mental-health, protected-characteristic, or intent profiling; or
- automated personal risk, trustworthiness, employability, or threat scores.

If a request mixes permitted and prohibited goals, offer the safe, bounded portion only.

## External-action boundary

Opening and reading a public page is collection. The following are external actions and need separate
authorization even when they are otherwise legitimate:

- contacting a source or subject;
- creating an account or research persona;
- following, connecting, subscribing, reacting, or posting;
- submitting a form;
- purchasing access;
- uploading a file or identifier to a third party; or
- requesting removal, correction, or disclosure from an external organization.

Do not take these actions as an implied part of an investigation.

## Data minimization and handling

- Store the minimum evidence needed to reproduce material findings.
- Prefer stable public URLs and short notes over copying whole profiles or datasets.
- Redact unrelated identifiers in reports and screenshots.
- Separate raw evidence from public-facing conclusions.
- Record why sensitive data was necessary when it cannot be avoided.
- Recommend deletion or review dates for retained personal data.
- Do not place sensitive evidence in a public repository, shared log, or external model prompt.
