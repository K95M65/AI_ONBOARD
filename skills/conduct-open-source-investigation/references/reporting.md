# Reporting

Choose the smallest report that answers the decision. A concise evidence brief is preferable to an
unnecessary personal dossier.

## Timeline

Add a timeline only when sequence changes the interpretation:

| Date or range | Event | Entity | Evidence | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Distinguish event date, publication date, and retrieval date.

## Entity map

Add an entity map only when relationships are central:

| Entity A | Relationship | Entity B | Evidence | Confidence | Collision risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Use neutral relationship labels. Do not imply family, ownership, employment, or coordination without direct
support.

## Writing rules

- Lead with the scoped answer, not the search process.
- Cite every material factual claim near the claim.
- Separate confirmed facts, analytical inference, and unresolved leads.
- Explain why sources are or are not independent.
- Use precise dates and qualify stale evidence.
- Avoid labels such as “suspicious,” “dangerous,” or “trustworthy” without a defined, legitimate standard.
- Do not speculate about personality, motive, mental health, protected characteristics, or private conduct.
- Redact unnecessary email addresses, phone numbers, home addresses, exact coordinates, family details, and
  other identifiers.
- State when access limits, source deletion, language, geography, or tooling constrain coverage.

## Review checklist

Before delivery, confirm:

- the report answers only the authorized questions;
- every material claim maps to evidence;
- search snippets are not presented as primary sources;
- identity collisions and contradictions are explicit;
- inference is labeled;
- confidence matches the evidence;
- personal data is minimized;
- no paid service, private source, bypass, or deceptive interaction was used;
- citations are accessible or their archival status is explained; and
- remaining gaps and stopping reasons are honest.
