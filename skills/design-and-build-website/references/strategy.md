# Strategy

Define the website's role before selecting layouts or visual styles.

## Inspect

- Read existing product, brand, analytics, customer, content, and technical material.
- Identify current pages, traffic sources, conversion paths, constraints, and failure points.
- Separate known facts from assumptions and research questions.
- Use external research only when the request needs current market, audience, or competitor evidence.

## Decide

Capture these decisions in a brief:

1. **Business objective:** State what the site must change for the organization.
2. **Primary audience:** Name the specific buyer, user, or stakeholder and their context.
3. **Audience job:** Complete, "When ___, they need to ___, so they can ___."
4. **Primary conversion:** Choose one dominant next action. List secondary actions separately.
5. **Value proposition:** State the valuable outcome, intended audience, and meaningful distinction.
6. **Proof:** Inventory demonstrations, metrics, customers, credentials, testimonials, and guarantees that
   actually exist.
7. **Success measure:** Choose observable measures such as qualified submissions, trials, purchases,
   bookings, or completed applications.
8. **Position and voice:** Define how the experience should feel and which familiar category conventions it
   may use or deliberately reject.
9. **Scope:** List pages, locales, integrations, content ownership, and launch target.
10. **Constraints:** Record the stack, brand system, accessibility target, performance budget, legal
    requirements, schedule, and hosting path.

## Prioritize

- Optimize the first screen for comprehension, not maximum information.
- Put the strongest audience problem and outcome ahead of implementation details.
- Match the conversion commitment to the visitor's likely readiness.
- Plan a proof path: claim → explanation → evidence → action.
- Remove pages and sections that do not support an audience job or required operational purpose.

## Gate

Proceed when each page has a named audience, job, primary action, and source of credible content. State
assumptions when the user has authorized autonomous progress; otherwise request the missing high-impact
decision.
