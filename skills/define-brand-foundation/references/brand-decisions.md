# Brand decisions

## Make traits operational

Define each trait as:

- **We are:** a behavior the brand demonstrates.
- **So we:** observable writing, design, product, or service choices.
- **But not:** the nearby excess or failure mode.

“Confident” might mean giving direct recommendations with evidence, but not overstating certainty. Avoid
lists of positive adjectives that cannot resolve a tradeoff.

## Define voice and tone

Voice remains recognizable; tone changes with user context. Specify tone for success, learning, purchase,
error, delay, sensitive data, safety, support, and incident communication. Pair rules with real examples and
anti-examples.

## Define visual principles

Connect each principle to strategy and application:

> Because the audience needs **[condition]**, expression should **[behavior]**, especially in **[contexts]**,
> while avoiding **[failure mode]**.

Cover hierarchy, typography, color, imagery, shape, iconography, motion, and density only to the depth that
the strategy can support. Preserve platform conventions where they improve trust, familiarity, or access.

## Choose architecture

Compare:

- **Branded house:** one master brand across offerings.
- **Endorsed brands:** distinct offerings visibly backed by a parent.
- **House of brands:** independent brands with limited public parent connection.
- **Hybrid:** an explicit, governed combination.

Base the choice on audience overlap, equity, risk, operational capacity, cross-sell value, and need for
distinct positioning. Architecture is an operating commitment, not a diagram alone.

## Govern proof

Maintain an approved evidence register for performance, scale, customer, certification, sustainability, and
comparative claims. Record source, scope, date, owner, expiration or review trigger, and permitted wording.
